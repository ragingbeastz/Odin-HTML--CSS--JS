Slow Cooker Beef Stew

Description

Slow Cooker Beef Stew is a hearty and comforting meal that is perfect for a cold day. It is made with tender beef, potatoes, carrots, and onions in a rich and flavorful broth. This recipe is easy to make and can be customized with your favorite ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef chuck, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 4 carrots, sliced
  • 4 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, to garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine beef, flour, salt, pepper, paprika, and thyme. Toss to coat.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Add beef and cook until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes.
  4. Transfer beef to slow cooker.
  5. Add onion and garlic to skillet and cook until softened, about 3 minutes.
  6. Stir in beef broth, red wine, and tomato paste. Bring to a boil, then pour over beef in slow cooker.
  7. Add carrots, potatoes, and bay leaf to slow cooker. Season with salt and pepper.
  8. Cover and cook on low for 8 hours or high for 4 hours, until beef is tender.
  9. Remove bay leaf before serving. Garnish with fresh parsley.
